3 """
4 RowVector(vector)
5
6 A lazy-view wrapper of an `AbstractVector`, which turns a length-`n` vector into a `1×n`
7 shaped row vector and represents the transpose of a vector (the elements are also transposed
8 recursively). This type is usually constructed (and unwrapped) via the [`transpose`](@ref)
9 function or `.'` operator (or related [`ctranspose`](@ref) or `'` operator).
10
11 By convention, a vector can be multiplied by a matrix on its left (`A * v`) whereas a row
12 vector can be multiplied by a matrix on its right (such that `v.' * A = (A.' * v).'`). It
13 differs from a `1×n`-sized matrix by the facts that its transpose returns a vector and the
14 inner product `v1.' * v2` returns a scalar, but will otherwise behave similarly.
15 """
